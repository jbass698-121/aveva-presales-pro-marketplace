# Battlecard: AspenTech (Emerson-owned)

## Threat Profile

- **Market position:** dominant process simulation and process design in refining and petrochemicals.
- **Corporate structure:** wholly-owned subsidiary of Emerson since March 12, 2025. No longer independent.
- **Revenue:** disclosed as part of Emerson; pre-acquisition AspenTech standalone ~$1.1B (FY2024).
- **Core strength:** Aspen Plus, Aspen HYSYS, Aspen DMC3 (advanced process control), Aspen InfoPlus.21 (operations data), Aspen Mtell (predictive maintenance).
- **Threat tier (this distributor):** primary in petrochemical and refining process design conversations; secondary in O&G operations where Emerson DeltaV is also in play.

## Product Portfolio

- **Aspen Plus / HYSYS** — process simulation (steady-state and dynamic)
- **Aspen DMC3 / Aspen APC** — advanced process control / multivariable control
- **Aspen InfoPlus.21** — process information management / historian (competitive with PI System in some accounts)
- **Aspen Mtell** — asset performance management with predictive maintenance
- **Aspen AIoT Hub** — IoT and AI-driven operations
- Now bundled into Emerson commercial motions alongside DeltaV.

## AVEVA Counter-Strategy

- **Primary positioning:** AVEVA, part of Schneider Electric — unified portfolio (process automation + electrical + energy + software-defined) vs. process-focused AspenTech.
- **Top differentiator #1:** AVEVA SimSci (now Process Simulator Suite) provides comparable process simulation and dynamic modeling with broader integration to System Platform + PI System operations data.
- **Top differentiator #2:** AVEVA's PI System has 75% O&G market penetration vs. Aspen InfoPlus.21's narrower deployment base.
- **Top differentiator #3:** AVEVA + ETAP + EAE + PME unified portfolio matches Emerson's DCS + AspenTech process modeling but adds electrical and software-defined automation Emerson doesn't have natively.

## When AVEVA Wins

- Customers wanting unified process + electrical + energy + automation (vs. process-only).
- Multi-industry operators (chemicals + power, chemicals + water utility) where AVEVA's breadth shows.
- Customers valuing AVEVA's PI System depth over Aspen InfoPlus.21.
- Customer is software-first / cloud-first; CONNECT cloud architecture vs. AspenAIoT Hub.

## When AspenTech (Emerson) Wins

- Refining and petrochemical process design where AspenTech's heritage is uncontested.
- Existing Aspen Plus / HYSYS process models drive integration choice.
- Customer is committed to Emerson stack; bundled DeltaV + Aspen + Plantweb is the operational reality.

## Top Objections + Responses

### Objection: "We use Aspen Plus / HYSYS for all our process design."
**Response:** "Process design and operations are different conversations. Aspen Plus is strong in design. AVEVA's strength is in operations — System Platform for SCADA, PI System for historian intelligence, AVEVA MES for batch operations. The two stacks coexist well; many customers run Aspen for design and AVEVA for operations. Operations is where the value compounds over decades."
**Gap-closure:** Show a hybrid architecture diagram. AVEVA does not displace Aspen Plus.

### Objection: "Aspen InfoPlus.21 is our historian."
**Response:** "InfoPlus.21 works for what it does. PI System has the deeper Asset Framework model and 75% market share among major O&G producers. The migration math from InfoPlus.21 to PI System has well-trodden patterns; we can show reference architectures."
**Gap-closure:** Migration assessment. Many AVEVA customers are former InfoPlus.21 users.

### Objection: "AspenTech is now part of Emerson — DeltaV + Aspen is one vendor."
**Response:** "True since March 2025. AVEVA's counter is the same logic on a broader scope: AVEVA, part of Schneider Electric, gives you process + electrical + software-defined + power monitoring under one MSA. Emerson + AspenTech is process design + DCS — narrower than the unified AVEVA portfolio. The right comparison is AVEVA's four pillars vs. Emerson's stack."
**Gap-closure:** Side-by-side portfolio scope comparison.
