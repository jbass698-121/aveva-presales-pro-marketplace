# Battlecard: Emerson

## Threat Profile

- **Market position:** strong incumbent in process industries, especially upstream O&G and chemicals.
- **Revenue:** Emerson Automation Solutions ~$4.2B (2023) — verify current.
- **Core strength:** DeltaV DCS + comprehensive instrumentation portfolio + Plantweb digital ecosystem + Aspen integration.
- **Threat tier (this distributor):** primary in upstream O&G; significant in midstream and chemicals.

## Product Portfolio

- DCS: DeltaV (modern generation)
- Instrumentation and final control elements (Rosemount, Fisher, Micro Motion, etc.)
- Plantweb digital ecosystem and analytics
- AMS Device Manager (asset management)
- AspenTech process simulation portfolio (Aspen Plus, HYSYS, etc.) — wholly-owned subsidiary since March 2025 take-private; integrated with DeltaV

## Their Competitive Advantages

1. Process optimization and reliability consulting heritage — Emerson sells solutions, not just products.
2. Complete measurement and final control element portfolio (Rosemount, Fisher, Micro Motion, Bettis).
3. DeltaV is widely regarded as easy to use and fast to commission for moderate-complexity DCS deployments.
4. Plantweb digital twin integration and AMS asset management have decade-plus track record.
5. AspenTech ownership (since March 2025) — Emerson now sells DeltaV + Aspen process modeling as one stack. AspenTech's $17B value reflects the strategic significance to Emerson's integrated digital play.

## AVEVA Counter-Strategy

- **Primary positioning:** complete industrial intelligence vs. process-only focus. AVEVA, part of Schneider Electric, covers process + energy + discrete in one architecture (AVEVA + Foxboro DCS + ETAP + EAE + PME). Emerson's AspenTech ownership gives it the process-modeling layer; AVEVA's unified portfolio still has the energy and discrete breadth Emerson doesn't match.
- **Top differentiator #1:** AVEVA's PI System historian breadth and 75% O&G market penetration — Plantweb's analytics is built on a much narrower foundation.
- **Top differentiator #2:** EcoStruxure energy management beyond DeltaV's process scope.
- **Top differentiator #3:** Multi-industry strength — AVEVA covers discrete, process, infrastructure, and energy in one platform.
- **Cloud / cloud-readiness story:** AVEVA Connect cloud architecture is broader and more mature than Plantweb cloud.

## When AVEVA Wins

- Multi-industry environments (e.g., integrated O&G + petrochem + power).
- Energy and sustainability are strategic mandates.
- Software-first or cloud-first requirements where Emerson's hardware-centric approach is a poor fit.
- Customers wanting one unified platform across process, discrete, and energy.

## When Emerson Wins

- Upstream O&G incumbency — DeltaV has a strong installed base in production operations.
- Customers prioritizing depth of instrumentation portfolio (Rosemount + Fisher + Micro Motion bundles).
- Greenfield process plants where Emerson's bundled hardware-software-services is the operating model.

## Top Objections + Responses

### Objection: "DeltaV and Plantweb provide all the process optimization we need."
**Response:** "Process optimization is important, and Foxboro DCS with AVEVA System Platform provides equivalent process control. The difference: Emerson focuses on process. AVEVA delivers process + energy + discrete in one platform. One stack for the complete facility."
**Gap-closure:** Show a live demo of an integrated facility — process + energy + discrete — running on AVEVA. Most Emerson customers haven't seen this depth.

### Objection: "We standardize on Rosemount and Fisher instruments."
**Response:** "Keep them. AVEVA System Platform integrates with Rosemount, Fisher, and Micro Motion via standard protocols (HART, Foundation Fieldbus, Profibus, OPC-UA). The instrument layer doesn't have to change — the operations and historian layer is where AVEVA brings the multi-vendor and multi-industry advantage."
**Gap-closure:** Reference architecture showing AVEVA on top of Rosemount-instrumented field.

### Objection: "DeltaV is easier to commission than other DCS platforms."
**Response:** "DeltaV is good for what it does. The harder question is what you do once it's running — when you need historian intelligence, multi-site rollup, energy optimization, recipe management, and cloud integration, the AVEVA stack on top is where the value compounds. Many DeltaV customers run AVEVA PI System and System Platform on top for exactly this reason."
**Gap-closure:** Hybrid architecture proposal — DeltaV preserved, AVEVA added on top.
