# AVEVA + ETAP + EAE + PME — The Unified Portfolio Narrative

This is the differentiated story that distinguishes Schneider Electric channel partners (and Schneider direct sellers) from AVEVA-only competitors. AVEVA-only distributors can sell AVEVA. Only Schneider-channel partners can sell the *integrated* portfolio.

## The narrative

**No competitor offers the unified orchestration of process automation, electrical systems, software-defined automation, and power management in a single, secure, hardware-agnostic platform — under one company.**

AVEVA is part of Schneider Electric (acquisition completed January 2023). The four pillars are not a partnership stack — they're one corporate portfolio under one MSA, one renewal motion, one support contract.

Customers running these four domains in silos pay a fragmentation tax — invisible cost in unplanned downtime, stranded electrical capacity, reactive maintenance, and energy waste. Unifying them on a model-driven platform creates compounding value at the operations layer.

## The four pillars

### 1. AVEVA — Industrial Intelligence Platform
- **AVEVA System Platform** (multi-vendor SCADA / HMI / supervisory control)
- **AVEVA InTouch HMI** (operator interface; InTouch Unlimited perpetual remains; subscription via Operations Control — Edge)
- **AVEVA Plant SCADA** (process SCADA, bundled in Operations Control — Supervisory)
- **AVEVA PI System** (industrial historian + Asset Framework + Event Frames; on-prem with PI Vision; cloud via PI Data Infrastructure with CONNECT Visualization)
- **AVEVA CONNECT** (industrial intelligence cloud platform): CONNECT data services, CONNECT Visualization, **AVEVA Industrial AI Assistant** (GA January 2026 — natural-language query, role-based permissions, never trained on customer data)
- **AVEVA Manufacturing Execution System** (IDC MarketScape MES Leader; batch + recipe + electronic batch records + OEE)
- **AVEVA Operations Control** subscription model — three packages: **Edge**, **Supervisory**, **Enterprise**
- **AVEVA Unified Operations Center** (bundled into Operations Control — Enterprise; multi-site orchestration)

**What it delivers:** real-time operations visibility, multi-site standardization, batch / recipe / OEE intelligence, regulatory reporting automation, **and embedded AI** (Industrial AI Assistant + Generative Design AI + Predictive Design AI + Intelligent Point Cloud Framework — all GA January 2026).

### 2. ETAP — Electrical Power Systems Digital Twin
- Load flow, short-circuit, motor starting analysis
- Protection coordination
- Arc flash analysis
- Real-time power monitoring (paired with PME)
- Microgrid design and operation
- Round-trip integration with AVEVA via the ETAP Integration Service

**What it delivers:** electrical design certainty, regulatory arc-flash compliance, energy optimization at the electrical layer.

### 3. EcoStruxure Automation Expert (EAE) — Software-Defined Automation
- IEC 61499 standard
- Hardware-independent process control
- Lifecycle reduction (design → simulation → deployment in days, not months)
- Vendor-agnostic execution layer

**What it delivers:** automation portability, faster engineering, future-proof control logic that survives hardware refresh.

### 4. Power Monitoring Expert (PME) — Power Quality and Energy
- Building-and-plant-level power monitoring
- Power quality analysis
- Energy management and Scope 2 reporting
- Integration with AVEVA System Platform via standard protocols

**What it delivers:** energy cost reduction, ESG reporting, power quality root-cause analysis.

## Strategic partnerships (extend the four pillars)

- **AVEVA + Databricks** (Strategic partnership 2024-04; Manufacturing ISV Partner of the Year 2025). CONNECT and Databricks share data via **Delta Sharing** — open standard, real-time, no ETL. The right answer for customers running an industrial DataOps / data-fabric strategy alongside the SCADA decision.

## How the four work together

The integration is not just "we sell all four." It's:

- **Common data fabric.** PI System ingests data from ALL four — process tags, electrical telemetry, EAE function-block status, PME power quality.
- **Unified visualization.** System Platform graphics surface electrical, process, and energy KPIs in one operator view.
- **Shared engineering layer.** EAE's IEC 61499 model is consumed by AVEVA's Operations Management Interface. ETAP's electrical model is referenced by System Platform's load views.
- **One commercial relationship.** Schneider channel partner; one MSA, one support contract, one renewal motion.

## How to use this narrative in artifacts

- **Industry playbooks:** the unified portfolio is a top-level section in every active industry playbook (post-Executive Summary, before Industry Segments). The specific pillar weighting varies by industry — Mining leads with electrical (mill drives, comminution energy); CPG leads with EAE software-defined; Water leads with AVEVA + PME for energy and compliance.

- **Strategic account briefs:** the unified portfolio is the framing in Part 2 (Schneider/AVEVA Solution Alignment). Each strategic priority gets mapped to specific pillar(s) with quantified opportunity sizing.

- **Executive decks:** the unified portfolio is typically slides 8-12 of a 20-slide deck — after the industry inflection / customer pain segment, as the solution reveal.

- **Quick references:** the unified portfolio fits in a 4-pillar mini-table on the cheat sheet.

## When NOT to lead with this

- Tier-1 cost-conscious customers who need a single specific product (e.g., "we just need an InTouch license"). Leading with the full portfolio overwhelms.
- Pure-software conversations where the customer doesn't care about electrical (rare in industrial automation).
- Customer is already deeply locked into Schneider hardware AND has rejected the AVEVA software pitch on prior engagements. Don't push the integrated story; pull through hardware on its merits.
