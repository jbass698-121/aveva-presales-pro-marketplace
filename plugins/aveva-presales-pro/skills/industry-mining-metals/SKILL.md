## The MMM thesis (executive framing)

The mining sector operates at an inflection point. Three forces converge:
1. **Declining ore grades** — modern mining processes 3-10× more raw material per unit metal than 20 years ago.
2. **ESG mandates clash with capital constraints** — net-zero commitments demand 3% annual energy intensity cuts, but capex is flat.
3. **Aging workforce** — experienced operators retiring; institutional knowledge leaving.

The fragmentation crisis: most mining companies run process automation, electrical systems, and power management in disconnected silos — SCADA, legacy DCS, separate historian, utility billing spreadsheets. Result: hidden inefficiency, reactive maintenance, energy waste, compliance risk.

## The unified portfolio answer

This is where AVEVA + ETAP + EAE + PME wins decisively:

- **AVEVA System Platform + PI System:** real-time operational visibility, comminution monitoring, mine-to-mill optimization.
- **ETAP:** electrical digital twin for mill drives, comminution circuits (which consume 25%+ of total mine energy).
- **EAE:** software-defined automation portable across pits and processing trains.
- **PME:** plant-level power monitoring, energy intensity per tonne reporting.

No competitor offers this integration natively. Rockwell, Siemens, ABB each offer pieces but not the unified orchestration.

## Sub-segments

- **Open-pit mining:** comminution, primary crushing, autonomous haulage; mining + electrical + energy stack matters.
- **Underground mining:** ventilation, hoist, drilling automation; safety + electrical reliability.
- **Mineral processing:** SAG/ball mills, flotation, leaching; energy intensity dominant.
- **Smelting / refining:** thermal processes, off-gas, environmental compliance; full unified portfolio.
